# Collaborative Minting User Guide

## What is Collaborative Minting?

Collaborative Minting allows multiple creators to work together on a single NFT, combining their unique styles, ideas, and contributions into one collaborative masterpiece. It's a social, creative experience that results in truly unique NFTs with shared ownership and rewards.

## Getting Started

### Finding Collaboration Sessions

1. **From Homepage**: Scroll to the "Collaborative Minting" showcase to see top 3 active sessions
2. **From Navigation**: Click "Collaborations" in the main navigation menu
3. **From Gallery**: Click the "Collaborate" button on any NFT to start a new session

### Session Information

Each collaboration session displays:
- **Collaboration Type**: The style or method of collaboration
- **Title**: Name of the collaboration
- **Description**: Goals and guidelines
- **Contributors**: Current vs. maximum contributors
- **Contribution Count**: Total contributions made
- **Time Remaining**: Deadline for contributions
- **Progress Bar**: Visual indicator of session completion

## Types of Collaborations

### 1. Sequential Collaboration
- Contributors add to the NFT one after another
- Each person builds on previous work
- Order matters
- Creates a narrative or evolution

**Best for**: Storytelling, progressive art, layered compositions

### 2. Parallel Collaboration
- All contributors work simultaneously
- Contributions are merged at the end
- Equal participation
- Democratic process

**Best for**: Diverse styles, experimental art, community projects

### 3. Layer-Based Collaboration
- Each contributor creates a specific layer
- Layers are combined to form final NFT
- Specialized roles (background, character, effects, etc.)
- Structured approach

**Best for**: Complex compositions, role-based teams, professional projects

### 4. Trait Voting Collaboration
- Community votes on traits to include
- Winning traits are combined
- Democratic decision-making
- Community-driven outcome

**Best for**: Community engagement, fair representation, diverse input

## Joining a Collaboration

### Prerequisites
- Connected wallet
- Verified account (for some sessions)
- Meeting any session-specific requirements

### Steps to Join

1. Navigate to the Collaborations page
2. Browse available sessions
3. Click "Join Session" on your chosen collaboration
4. Review the collaboration guidelines
5. Confirm participation
6. Wait for your turn (sequential) or start contributing (parallel)

### Contribution Guidelines

When contributing to a collaboration:
- **Respect the Theme**: Follow the session's creative direction
- **Quality Over Speed**: Take time to create meaningful contributions
- **Communicate**: Use session chat to coordinate with others
- **Be Original**: Add your unique perspective
- **Meet Deadlines**: Submit before the contribution deadline

## Creating a Collaboration Session

### Steps to Create

1. Go to Collaborations page
2. Click "Create New Session"
3. Fill in session details:
   - **Title**: Clear, descriptive name
   - **Description**: Goals, theme, and guidelines
   - **Collaboration Type**: Choose from available types
   - **Max Contributors**: Set participant limit (2-20)
   - **Contribution Deadline**: Set time limit
   - **Visibility**: Public or invite-only
4. Set collaboration rules (optional):
   - Minimum contribution quality
   - Specific trait requirements
   - Style guidelines
5. Confirm and create session

### Session Settings

**Public Sessions**
- Anyone can join
- Visible to all users
- Great for community building
- Higher participation

**Invite-Only Sessions**
- Requires invitation link
- Controlled participant list
- Better for specific teams
- More focused collaboration

**Contribution Limits**
- Set minimum/maximum contributions per user
- Control session pace
- Ensure fair participation
- Prevent spam

### Contribution Review Process

1. **Submit Contribution**: Upload your work
2. **Peer Review**: Other contributors can review (optional)
3. **Voting Period**: Contributors vote to accept or request changes
4. **Integration**: Accepted contributions are merged
5. **Finalization**: Session creator approves final NFT

### Quality Standards

Contributions should meet these standards:
- **Resolution**: Minimum 1000x1000 pixels
- **Format**: PNG, JPG, or SVG
- **File Size**: Under 10MB
- **Originality**: No copyrighted content
- **Relevance**: Matches session theme

## Ownership and Rewards

### Shared Ownership

Collaborative NFTs have unique ownership models:

**Equal Split**
- All contributors own equal shares
- Royalties divided equally
- Democratic decision-making
- Fair for equal contributions

**Weighted Split**
- Ownership based on contribution amount/quality
- More contributions = larger share
- Incentivizes quality and quantity
- Fair for varied participation

**Creator-Led**
- Session creator owns majority
- Contributors receive smaller shares
- Clear leadership
- Good for directed projects

### Royalty Distribution

When a collaborative NFT is sold:
1. Sale price is recorded
2. Platform fee is deducted (typically 2.5%)
3. Remaining amount is split according to ownership model
4. Each contributor receives their share automatically
5. Payments sent to contributor wallets

### Contribution Credits

All contributors are credited:
- Listed in NFT metadata
- Visible on NFT detail page
- Included in blockchain record
- Permanent attribution

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How many collaborations can I join?
Unlimited! You can participate in as many sessions as you want simultaneously.

### Can I leave a collaboration?
Yes, before the contribution deadline. After that, your contributions are locked in.

### What if a contributor doesn't participate?
Sessions have minimum participation requirements. Inactive contributors may be removed.

### Can I collaborate with specific people?
Yes! Create an invite-only session and share the link with your chosen collaborators.

### How long do sessions last?
Session creators set the duration, typically ranging from 24 hours to 2 weeks.

### Can I sell my share of a collaborative NFT?
Yes, you can sell your ownership share to others through the marketplace.

### What happens if contributors disagree?
The session creator has final say, or you can use voting mechanisms to resolve disputes.

### Are there collaboration limits?
Maximum 20 contributors per session to ensure quality and manageability.
